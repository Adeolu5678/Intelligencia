# Homepage Redesign Rules

This document captures the redesign rules established from the new homepage implementation. It is intentionally independent from the previous `reference-page-system.md`.

## Shared Design Direction

- Tone: editorial-enterprise, premium, restrained, deliberate
- Quality signal: composition, proportion, spacing, material control
- Avoid: giant headline domination, repetitive card grids, generic agency polish, decorative excess

## Structural Rules

- Major desktop sections should feel like contained experiences, typically between `72svh` and `90svh`, not automatic full-screen slabs.
- The site should maintain one controlled background field across the page, with subtle panel boundaries rather than hard section breaks.
- Each page should use a common shell and material language, but the middle sections must vary in structure.
- Approved section archetypes introduced here:
  - hero with copy stage + media/metric aside + service rail
  - strategic statement + vertical principle rail
  - asymmetric service composition with one wide feature and two supporting cards
  - featured proof block + compact proof cards + testimonial stack
  - qualification statement + contained action panel

## Hierarchy Rules

- Typography should be assertive but not oversized relative to container width.
- Eyebrows should be used sparingly and only where they establish sequence or context.
- Dead space should be resolved with secondary information, service rails, captions, or proof panels rather than decorative filler.

## Layout Rules For Later Pages

- Preserve the outward-balanced container width.
- Keep image usage limited to mapped slots.
- Reuse the section archetype logic, not the exact homepage arrangement.
- Any later page should combine at least two distinct middle-section archetypes rather than repeating one system throughout.
